B1.3 Language focus: Adverbs of frequency
Adverbs of frequency tell us how often something happens.
Example:
Amare is often late for school and he is never early for anything.
He is late every day of the week.
Work in pairs and use sometimes, often, never, every day, once a week, regularly to ask and
answer questions about how often you:
a … are late for school.
b … play sports after school.
c … forget your homework.
d … eat fresh vegetables.
e … go to bed after midnight.
f … play with your friends.
B1.4 Listening: Keeping fit
1
Listen to your teacher read part of a lecture on the importance of health. Take notes about
the main points.
2
Now answer the questions below.
a What sports do you play?
b How do you keep fit?
c What is your favourite meal?
d Why do you need sleep?
3
With the class, brainstorm your ideas for keeping fit.
B1.5 Speaking: Health and fitness
Discuss in groups the importance of health and fitness and prepare a presentation on one of
the following topics: Health, diet, fitness, hygiene.
